Mars

The fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System.

About Mars

Mars, frequently called the "Red Planet," owes its distinct crimson hue to the pervasive iron oxide—essentially rust that coats its surface. Today, water remains locked away as subterranean ice and polar caps of frozen water and dry ice, serving as a primary focal point for the ongoing search for signs of ancient microbial life and the potential for future human colonization.

Key Facts

Similar Day/Night Cycle To Earth

A day on Mars is roughly 24 hours and 40 minutes, similar to Earth, allowing for human-friendly circadian rhythms

Largest Volcano In Our Solar System

Mars is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the solar system, which is about three times taller than Mount Everest.

Mars Used To Be Filled With Water

While it is a desert world today, Mars has vast amounts of water ice at its poles.
